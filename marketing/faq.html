Frequently Asked Questions

Is my writing really private?

Your entries are stored directly on your device in a folder you choose. We have no servers and no way to access your data.

If you use the AI analysis feature, your entry text is sent to Anthropic or OpenAI (whichever you configure) - but using your own API key, not ours. This means it goes directly to your AI provider account, not through any service we control. The AI providers don't store API requests for training.

What browsers are supported?

Dumpster Fire is desktop only and requires a Chromium-based browser: Chrome, Edge, Brave, or Arc (version 86 or later). This is because we use the File System Access API to read and write files directly on your device.

Safari, Firefox, and mobile browsers don't support this API, so the app won't work there.

Where is my data stored?

Your entries are saved as plain markdown files (.md) in a folder you select. Metadata like word count and analysis results are stored in companion .meta.json files. You can open these files in any text editor.

The folder structure looks like: entries/2026/01/2026-01-15-1.md

How does AI analysis work?

When you hit your word goal and "strike the match," the app sends your entry text to either Anthropic (Claude) or OpenAI (GPT) - whichever you've configured. The AI identifies themes, mood, and patterns in your writing, then returns a summary.

You need to provide your own API key, which is stored encrypted on your device. The AI providers have their own privacy policies, but your entries are not stored or used for training by either provider when using the API.

Can I use it offline?

Yes! Dumpster Fire is a Progressive Web App (PWA). Once you've loaded it once, you can install it and use it offline. The only feature that requires internet is AI analysis.

What's with the fire theme?

The concept is inspired by the therapeutic practice of writing unsent letters and burning them. You write freely (spill the ink), symbolically release the words (light it up), and gain clarity from what emerges (what remains). The fire animations are just for fun - your entries aren't actually deleted!

Why 750 words?

750 words is roughly three pages of writing - a length that's long enough to get past surface thoughts and into something meaningful, but short enough to be doable daily. It's the default, but you can change your goal in settings.

The concept comes from 750words.com, which inspired this project.

Is this free?

The app itself is completely free and open source. If you want to use AI analysis, you'll need your own API key from Anthropic or OpenAI, which have their own pricing (typically a few cents per analysis).

Can I sync across devices?

Not directly in the app, but since your entries are plain files, you can store them in a synced folder (Dropbox, iCloud Drive, Google Drive, etc.) and access them from any desktop with a supported browser.
